    Circular_Flow.owner_consumption¶

    flow

    Units: b$/year · Dimensions: Run

    Forced climate-related relocation spending is treated as owner consumption expenditure. Even though it is not voluntary consumption (like food, leisure, electronics), it is still a HHs expense that reduces their savings and affects their ability to consume other goods. Additionally, Owner households represent the wealthy asset-owning segment.

    Equation: 

    Circular_Flow.actual_owner_consumption+Circular_Flow.Actual_people_retreat_cost_from_SLR

{MAX(smoothed_owner_net_income*Circular_Flow.minimum_fraction_of_income_to_consumption;
(smoothed_owner_net_income*Circular_Flow.fraction_of_owner_income_to_consumption)*(1+Inflation.inflation)-Circular_Flow.annual_owner_savings_to_reach_goal)

{MAX (adjusted_owner_consumption; Circular_Flow.net_owner_income*minimum_fraction_of_owner_income_to_consumption)

    Circular_Flow.worker_savings_interest¶

    flow

    Units: B$/year · Dimensions: Run

    Workers earn interest income on their savings. Households deposit their savings in banks. Banks pay them a safe interest rate on these deposits.

    The worker savings earn interest equal to the average safe private debt interest rate, because: - Banks pay a lower, stable interest on deposits. - Deposits are considered safe liabilities. - A smoother, slow-moving rate prevents unrealistic volatility in household income.

    Thus, worker interest income reflects a delayed and damped response to monetary policy.

    Equation: 

    Circular_Flow.Worker_Savings*Finance.average_private_debt_safe_interest

    Units: b$/year · Dimensions: Run

    Insurance taxation takes a small percentage of owner wealth each year (after the policy starts) to fund government-backed climate insurance, reducing financial instability. The model includes it because owners hold most wealth and much of the exposed asset base, so they are treated as the main contributors to the insurance scheme.
