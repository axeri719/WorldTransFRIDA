    Flows¶

    Concrete.new_RS_construction¶

    flow Calibrated

    Units: Mtonne/year · Dimensions: Run

    This is the construction of new residential and service buildings measured in million tonnes of concrete per year. It is computed as the gap between existing and desired RS floor area, multiplied by the concrete intensity of floor area and divided by the average adjustment time for closing this gap.

    Equation: 

    Concrete.gap_in_useful_floor_area*Concrete.CONCRETE_PER_m2_UFA/Concrete.GAP_ADJUSTMENT_TIME_FOR_BUILDINGS

    Concrete.new_infrastructure_construction¶

    flow

    Units: Mtonne/year · Dimensions: Run

    This is the construction of new infrastructure measured in million tonnes of concrete per year. It is computed as the gap between existing and desired infrastructure divided by the average adjustment time for closing this gap. The rate is bounded by zero to avoid negative construction (demolition of existing young infrastructures).

    Equation: 

    MAX(0, 
Concrete.infrastructure_gap/
Concrete.GAP_ADJUSTMENT_TIME_FOR_INFRASTRUCTURE)

    Concrete.RS_aging¶

    flow

    Units: Mtonne/year · Dimensions: Run

    This is the rate determining how much of new Residential and Service buildings transition to the stock of old RS buildings each year. The flow is computed as the stock value of New RS buildings divided by the average lifetime of new RS buildings.

    Equation: 

    Concrete.Concrete_Stock_in_New_Residential_and_Service_Buildings/(Concrete.AVERAGE_LIFETIME_OF_BUILDINGS)*Concrete.AGE_CUTPOINT_FOR_CONCRETE_STRUCTURES

    Concrete.Old_RS_deterioration¶

    flow

    Units: Mtonne/year · Dimensions: Run

    This flow of concrete represents the deterioration of old residential and service buildings due to wear and tear plus additional damages due to extreme weather events.

    The flow is computed as the existing stock multiplied by the NORMAL CONCRETE DETERIORATION RATE parameter plus storm damages to service and residential buildings. The Storm damages are computed as the total storm damages to concrete buildings and infrastructure multiplied by the share of old concrete in the system that is used in residential and service buildings.

    Equation: 

    Concrete.Concrete_Stock_in_Old_Residential_and_Service_Buildings*
Concrete.NORMAL_CONCRETE_DETERIORATION_RATE

+

Concrete.storm_damages_to_concrete_buildings_and_infrastructure * (1-Concrete.share_of_old_concrete_in_infrastructure)

    Concrete.indicated_desired_per_capita_floor_area¶

    aux Assumption

    Units: Mm2/Mp · Dimensions: Run

    The effect of wealth (GDP per cap) on desired UFA is based on data on average UFA and GDP from European countries.

    The functional form of the relationship was derived by regressing UFA/cap on GDP/cap. Parameter were then calibrated to match historical concrete use in Residential and Service buildings.

    UFA per capita data: https://entranze.enerdata.net/average-floor-area-per-capita.html

    GDP per capita data: https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/gdp-per-capita-maddison?time=2008®ion=Europe

    Equation: 

    MAX(
0, 
Concrete.UFA_alpha*LN(Demographics.real_GDP_per_person*1000) + Concrete.UFA_intercept)

    Concrete.CLIMATE_IMPACT_ON_CONCRETE_LIFETIME¶

    aux Climate-impact parameter

    Units: dmnl/deg C · Dimensions: Run · Range: -0.15 – -0.05

    Climate change impacts the durability and lifetime of concrete structures through carbonation-induced corrosion and chloride-induced corrosion, driven by multiple temperature, CO2 and chloride exposure mechanisms.

    Global scale effects are hard to estimate, therefore a ball-park estimate of STA effect on reinforced concrete structure service life where 1 C increase ~ 5-15% reduction in service lifetime has been used.

    This qualitative estimate is derived from:

    Bastidas-Arteaga, E., & Stewart, M. G.: Damage risks and economic assessment of climate adaptation strategies for design of new concrete structures subject to chloride-induced corrosion, Structural Safety, 52, 40-53, 2015. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.strusafe.2014.10.005

    Stewart, M. G., Wang, X., and Nguyen, M-N.,.: Climate change impact and risks of concrete infrastructure deterioration, Engineering Structures, 33(4), 1326-1337. 2011. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.engstruct.2011.01.010

    Wang, X., Stewart, M.G., and Nguyen, M,: Impact of climate change on corrosion and damage to concrete infrastructure in Australia, Climatic Change, 110,3, 941-957, https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10584-011-0124-7

    Equation: 

    [1] = -0.075

    IF (Climate.switch_concrete_impact = 0) AND TIME > Climate.climate_impact_stoptime THEN
