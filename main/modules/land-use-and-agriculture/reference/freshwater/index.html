    Freshwater.available_water_supply¶

    stock Calibrated

    Units: m^3 · Dimensions: Run · Inflows: Freshwater.natural_water_recharge_flow, Freshwater.technological_water_discovery · Outflows: Freshwater.total_water_withdrawal

    From Billy and Lan: This is water that can be used by humans for ag (irrigation), domestic, industry

    This is after water has been reserved for ecological systems. If we want to modify that amount, we would add an outflow, and control that as a policy.

    Water is withdrawn by people (for drinking and industry) using GDP per person to model the per person consumption for those purposes then multiplying by Population

    Water is withdrawn for irrigation based on a fraction of cropland irrigated (which itself is driven off of relative water availability. Water withdrawn (i.e. lost) on a per unit of crop production basis is driven by technology (down), climate (up).

    Irrigation effects crop yield, but does not set it to 0 when the available water supply runs out, because of rain.

    The available water supply is recharged at a natural constant rate (safe assumption re: Lan). It can also be supplemented by humans who "drill deeper" via technological water discovery, and the pressure to increase that, as water supplies dwindle.
