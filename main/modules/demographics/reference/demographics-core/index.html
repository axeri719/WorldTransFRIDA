    Demographics.Births¶

    flow Calibrated

    Units: Mp/Year · Dimensions: Run

    Births are based on a calculated fertility rate, the result of a calibrated balancing power function regression using GDP per person as an input. With rising standards of living and education, birth rates drop exponentially (Callegari & Stoknes, 2023; Kirk, 1996; Lesthaeghe, 2010; Marquez-Ramos and Mourelle, 2019, Proto and Rustichini, 2013).

    Callegari B, Stoknes PE. 2023. People and Planet: 21st- century sustainable population scenarios and possible living standards within planetary boundaries. Earth4All, version 1.0.

    Kirk D. 1996. Demographic transition theory. Population Studies 50(3): 361–387. https://doi.org/10. 1080/0032472031000149536.

    Lesthaeghe, R. 2010. The unfolding story of the second demographic transition. Population and Development Review 36(2): 211–251. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1728-4457.2010.00328.x.

    Marquez-Ramos L, Mourelle E. 2019. Education and economic growth: an empirical analysis of nonlinearities", Applied Economic Analysis 27 (79): 21-45. https://doi.org/10.1108/AEA-06-2019-0005

    Proto E, Rustichini A. 2013. A reassessment of the relationship between GDP and life satisfaction. PLoS One 8(11):e79358. doi: 10.1371/journal.pone.0079358.

    Equation: 

    (Demographics.number_of_fertile_women*Demographics.fertility)/Demographics.fertile_period

    Demographics.probability_to_reach_age_per_age¶

    aux

    Units: dmnl · Dimensions: Run, Ages

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_expectancy#Calculation

    Equation: 

    IF Ages = 1 
THEN 1-Demographics."0_death_rate"[Run]*Demographics.time_to_apply_death_rate

ELSE IF Ages > 1 AND Ages <= 20 
THEN (SELF[Run,1])*(1-Demographics."1_to_20_death_rate"[Run]*Demographics.time_to_apply_death_rate)^(Ages-1)

ELSE IF Ages > 20 AND Ages <= 40 
THEN (SELF[Run,20])*(1-Demographics."20_to_40_death_rate"[Run]*Demographics.time_to_apply_death_rate)^(Ages-20)

ELSE IF Ages > 40 AND Ages <= 60
THEN (SELF[Run,40])*(1-Demographics."40_to_60_death_rate"[Run]*Demographics.time_to_apply_death_rate)^(Ages-40)

ELSE IF Ages > 60 AND Ages <= 65
THEN (SELF[Run,60])*(1-Demographics."60_to_65_death_rate"[Run]*Demographics.time_to_apply_death_rate)^(Ages-60)

ELSE IF Ages > 65 AND Ages <= 75
THEN (SELF[Run,65])*(1-Demographics."65_to_75_death_rate"[Run]*Demographics.time_to_apply_death_rate)^(Ages-65)

ELSE IF Ages > 75
THEN (SELF[Run,75])*(1-Demographics.Over_75_death_rate[Run]*Demographics.time_to_apply_death_rate)^(Ages-75)

ELSE 0
