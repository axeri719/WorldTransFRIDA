    Biosphere_Indicators.grazing_land_net_primary_production¶

    aux

    Units: GtC/Year · Dimensions: Run

    Total actual net primary production of grazing lands.

    Equation: 

    Land_Use.grazing_land * Grass.grass_net_primary_production

    Biosphere_Indicators.Human_appropriation_of_net_primary_production¶

    aux

    Units: GtC/Year · Dimensions: Run

    Human appropriation of net primary production, defined as the sum of the NPP harvested or destroyed, the change in NPP due to land use and the human-induced change in NPP consumed by fires.

    Source: Haberl et al., 2007 (https://www.pnas.org/doi/full/10.1073/pnas.0704243104) and Stenzel et al., 2024 (https://gmd.copernicus.org/articles/17/3235/2024)

    Equation: 

    Biosphere_Indicators.HANPP_from_harvest_or_destruction + Biosphere_Indicators.HANPP_from_land_use_change + Biosphere_Indicators.HANPP_from_fires

    Biosphere_Indicators.HANPP_as_fraction_of_Holocene_net_primary_production¶

    aux

    Units: % · Dimensions: Run

    Control variable for the functional Biosphere Integrity planetary boundary, defined as human appropriation of net primary production as a fraction of the Holocene net primary production.

    Source: Richardson et al., 2023 (https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.adh2458)

    Equation: 

    Biosphere_Indicators.Human_appropriation_of_net_primary_production / Biosphere_Indicators.Holocene_net_primary_production * Biosphere_Indicators.UNIT_Percent_to_decimal

    Biosphere_Indicators."Fraction_of_NPP_consumed_by_human-induced_fires"¶

    aux

    Units: dmnl

    Fraction of total actual net primary production which is consumed by human-induced fires. It is assumed that this fraction is constant over time, and equals the ratio between the net primary production consumed by human-induced fires in year 2000 and the total actual net primary production in year 2000.

    Equation: 

    Biosphere_Indicators."Total_NPP_consumed_by_human-induced_fires_2000" / Biosphere_Indicators.Total_actual_NPP_2000

    Biosphere_Indicators."Ratio_N:P_in_man_made_fertilizer_2000"¶

    aux

    Units: MtN/MtP · Range: 8.6 – 8.6

    Mass ratio of nitrogen in synthetic fertilizers and nitrogen fixation to phosphorus in synthetic fertilizers. Calculated as ratio = (N fertilizer + N fixation) / P fertilizer with the values below from Bouwman et al., giving a value of 8.6 MtN/MtP, in line with the same calculation by Steffen et al (2015 SI).

    N fertilizer: 83 TgN/yr N fixation: 39 TgN/yr P fertilizer: 14 TgP/yr

    Value: 8.6 Source: Bouwman et al., 2011 (https://www.pnas.org/doi/full/10.1073/pnas.1012878108) and Steffen et al., 2015 (https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1259855)

    Equation: 

    8.6

    Biosphere_Indicators.P_flow_from_fertilizers_to_erodible_soils¶

    aux

    Units: MtP/year · Dimensions: Run

    Control variable for the regional phosphorus Biogeochemical Flows planetary boundary, defined as the mass flow of phosphorus from fertilizers to erodible soils.

    Assumptions: All agricultural soils are erodible with respect to P flow to freshwater (Steffen et al., 2015 SI)

    Source: Richardson et al., 2023 (https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.adh2458) and Steffen et al., 2015 (https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1259855)

    Equation: 

    (Land_Nutrients.man_made_fertilizer_use + Biosphere_Indicators.Intended_biological_nitrogen_fixation) / Biosphere_Indicators."Ratio_N:P_in_man_made_fertilizer_2000"

    Biosphere_Indicators.HANPP_from_harvest_or_destruction¶
