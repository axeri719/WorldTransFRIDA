    Fertilizer_Demand.Energy_factor¶

    aux Assumption

    Units: Dimensionless

    The parameter represents the additional energy required in fertilizer production beyond the carbon used as feedstock. In conventional ammonia production via steam methane reforming (SMR), roughly two-thirds of natural gas is used as feedstock and one-third is combusted to supply process heat, implying total natural gas input of about 1.5× the feedstock requirement. This relationship is consistent with estimates reported in the literature on ammonia production technologies. References: Karibe, H., Sair, S., Faik, A., & Ait Ousaleh, H. (2025). Electrified steam methane reforming: A review of heating technologies, challenges, and prospects. International Journal of Hydrogen Energy, 133, 200–213. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijhydene.2025.04.475 Rouwenhorst, K. H. R., Krzywda, P. M., Benes, N. E., Mul, G., & Lefferts, L. (2021). Ammonia production technologies. In A. Valera-Medina & R. Banares-Alcantara (Eds.), Techno-economic challenges of green ammonia as an energy vector (pp. 41–83). Elsevier. https://doi.org/10.1016/B978-0-12-820560-0.00004-7 Synthesis Gas reformers https://www.owlnet.rice.edu/~ceng403/nh3ref97.html
