    Inflation.Inflation_Index¶

    stock

    Units: dmnl · Dimensions: Run · Inflows: Inflation.change_in_inflation_index

    Inflation index (often called a price index or CPI index) is a cumulative measure of how much price have changed over time. E.g., if the inflation index = 1.2, the price today is 20% higher than in the base year.

    “What is the overall price level today compared to the base year?”

    it is used to compute the real GDP

    Initial value: 

    Inflation.initial_inflation_index

    Inflation.Inflation_Rate¶

    stock

    Units: 1/year · Dimensions: Run · Inflows: Inflation.inflationary_pressure · Outflows: Inflation.deflationary_pressure

    Inflation rate is the percentage change in prices per year. If it is > 0, then the prices are rising. If it is < 0, the prices are falling (deflation).

    “How fast are prices increasing right now?”

    Initial value: 

    Inflation.initial_inflation_rate

    Flows¶

    Inflation.change_in_inflation_index¶

    flow

    Units: 1/year · Dimensions: Run

    The inflation index increases at a rate equal to the inflation rate times the current index.

    Inflation changes the current price level, not the base year price level. Inflation is exponential (compounding), not linear.

    Equation: 

    Inflation.Inflation_Rate*Inflation.Inflation_Index

{IF TIME > 2020 THEN inflation_percentage ELSE 0,1-RAMP(,002; STARTTIME)

    Inflation.inflationary_pressure¶

    flow

    Units: 1/year/Year · Dimensions: Run

    If the inflation gap is positive, inflation must rise. The rise is gradual, over the inflation_adjustment_time. Only positive gaps create inflationary pressure.

    The MAX(0, …) ensures equation does not treat a negative gap as “negative pressure”; that is handled separately.

    Equation: 

    MAX(0, Inflation.inflation_gap/Inflation.inflation_adjustment_time)

    Inflation.deflationary_pressure¶

    flow

    Units: dmnl/year^2 · Dimensions: Run

    If the inflation gap is negative, inflation should fall. This negative adjustment happens gradually. Only negative gaps trigger deflation.

    MIN(0, …) isolates the negative part. The negative sign makes the outflow positive.

    Equation: 

    -MIN(0, Inflation.inflation_gap/Inflation.deflation_adjustment_time)

    aux

    Units: b$/year · Dimensions: Run

    It is the total income available to the whole economy (all sectors combined). It includes income generated and paid by the private sector AND net injections or withdrawals from the government sector

    Equation: 

    Inflation.private_income+Inflation.net_government_expenses

    Inflation.net_government_expenses¶

    aux

    Units: b$/year · Dimensions: Run

    Net government expenses represent the government’s net injection or withdrawal of money into the private economy. When it spends → it injects income. When it taxes → it withdraws income When it provides bailouts → it injects additional income

    If government_spending > tax_income → government is injecting money → aggregate income rises.

    Equation: 

    Government.public_expenditure-Government.total_public_tax_income +Government.bailouts

    aux

    Units: 1/year · Dimensions: Run

    Inflation gap is the difference between: (1) what inflation should be, based on cost pressures and economic factors (2) the current inflation rate.

    Inflation."inflation_adjustment_b$_per_bc$"¶

    aux

    Units: b$/bc$ · Dimensions: Run

    “How many nominal dollars correspond to one constant 2021 dollar?”

    Inflation Index = the cumulative price level relative to the base year (2021). unit_b$_per_bc$ = a unit-conversion constant (1 nominal B$ per 1 constant bc$ in the base year).

    If Inflation Index = 1.30 → inflation adjustment = 1.30 nominal B$ per 1 bc$.

    Equation: 

    Inflation.Inflation_Index*Inflation."unit_b$_per_bc$"

    Inflation.energy_supply_growth_rate¶

    The equation computes the smoothed annual growth rate of total energy output. It tells you how fast energy supply is increasing or decreasing, averaged over one year.

    Inflation.energy_market_balance¶

    aux

    Units: dmnl/year · Dimensions: Run

    It measures whether energy demand is growing faster or slower than energy supply. If demand grows faster than supply → energy becomes scarce, price pressure upward. If supply grows faster than demand → energy becomes abundant, downward pressure on cost/ price.

    Equation: 

    Inflation.energy_demand_growth_rate[Run]-Inflation.energy_supply_growth_rate[Run]
